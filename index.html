🆘 Emergency Guides

🩸
Severe Bleeding
Click to expand steps
Control bleeding immediately to prevent shock and save lives.
1
Call emergency services immediately if bleeding is severe
2
Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage
3
Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible
4
Do not remove the cloth if blood soaks through; add more layers
5
Apply a tourniquet only as last resort for life-threatening bleeding
🔥
Burns
Click to expand steps
Treat thermal, chemical, and electrical burns properly.
1
Remove the source of heat and ensure scene safety
2
Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for 10-20 minutes
3
Remove tight items before swelling occurs
4
Cover with sterile, non-stick bandage or clean cloth
5
Do NOT apply ice, butter, or cream to severe burns
💔
CPR & Choking
Click to expand steps
Life-saving techniques for cardiac arrest and airway obstruction.
1
Check responsiveness and call emergency services
2
For choking: Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
3
For CPR: Push hard and fast in center of chest (100-120/min)
4
Give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions
5
Continue until help arrives or person recovers
🤕
Head Injuries
Click to expand steps
Recognize signs of concussion and traumatic brain injury.
1
Stabilize head and neck; avoid movement
2
Check for bleeding and apply gentle pressure if needed
3
Monitor for vomiting, confusion, or loss of consciousness
4
Apply cold pack to reduce swelling
5
Seek immediate medical attention for severe symptoms
🦴
Fractures & Sprains
Click to expand steps
Immobilize injuries to prevent further damage.
1
Do not attempt to straighten or realign the limb
2
Immobilize the joint above and below the injury
3
Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling
4
Elevate if possible to reduce swelling
5
Check circulation below injury (color, warmth, pulse)
🐝
Allergic Reactions
Click to expand steps
Handle anaphylaxis and severe allergic responses.
1
Recognize signs: difficulty breathing, swelling, hives
2
Call emergency services immediately for severe reactions
3
Use epinephrine auto-injector if available and prescribed
4
Keep person lying down with legs elevated
5
Loosen tight clothing and cover with blanket

🎒 Kit Maintenance Tips

Check Expiry Dates: Review your kit every 6 months and replace expired medications and supplies.

Storage: Keep in a cool, dry place out of reach of children but easily accessible to adults.

Customize: Add personal medications, emergency contact numbers, and medical history forms.

Restock: Immediately replace items after use to ensure readiness.
